Both answers seem reasonable — which is exactly why maths needs a set of rules!

If you got 21: you added first (left to right). That feels natural, but it's not the rule.
If you got 11: you multiplied first. Multiplication comes before addition — that's the rule we'll learn today.

Without an agreed order, everyone gets a different answer. That's why we have BEDMAS / PEMDAS!

📋 Introduction · 10 minutes
The Rules of the Game
BEDMAS (or PEMDAS) is the agreed order everyone follows so maths always has one correct answer.
B
Brackets / Parentheses
Solve everything inside ( ) first — always.

Left-to-right rule for equal-priority operations
Division & Multiplication have equal priority — work left to right. Same rule applies to Addition & Subtraction. Example: 10 − 3 + 2 = 7 + 2 = 9 (NOT 10 − 5 = 5)

💬 Discussion Question
Why do you think a calculator can always give the correct answer to 5 + 2 × 3 without needing you to press = in between each step?
It's programmed to follow BEDMAS!

Calculators are built with BEDMAS rules hard-coded into their logic. When you type an expression and press =, the calculator doesn't just read left-to-right — it first scans for brackets, then exponents, then × and ÷, and finally + and −.

This is the same reason programming languages like Python, JavaScript, and Scratch all follow BEDMAS — so computers and humans always agree on one correct answer.
