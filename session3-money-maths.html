Real-World Money Maths

Three types of money problems that use multiplication

⏱ 10 mins
🛒

Repeated Purchases

Buying several identical items. Multiply the price by the number of items.

price × quantity
📱

Subscriptions

Paying a fixed amount regularly — monthly or yearly. How much over time?

cost × periods
🤝

Splitting Bills

Sharing a total cost equally. Uses division, but often linked to multiplication.

total ÷ people
✍️
Always write a number sentence first!
Before calculating, identify what you know and write it as a multiplication. This helps you avoid mistakes and shows your thinking clearly. Example: "3 tickets × $28.75 = ?"

🔑 Key Strategies for Decimal Multiplication:

COLUMN METHOD

Ignore the decimal, multiply whole numbers, then place the decimal back in.

BREAKING APART

Split the price into dollars and cents: $12.99 → ($12 × 12) + ($0.99 × 12)

Mixed Money Problems

Write the number sentence, then solve! Click a card to reveal the answer.

⏱ 15 mins
Problem 1 · Party Pizzas 🍕
You are organising a class party. You need 25 small pizzas, and each costs $10.50. What is the total cost?
$10.50 × 25 = ?
$262.50 🍕
Think: $10 × 25 = $250, then $0.50 × 25 = $12.50. Add: $262.50
Problem 2 · Concert Tickets 🎸
Three friends went to a concert. Each ticket cost $28.75. How much did they pay in total?
$28.75 × 3 = ?
$86.25 🎸
$28 × 3 = $84, then $0.75 × 3 = $2.25. Add: $86.25
Problem 3 · School Bus Pass 🚌
A school bus pass costs $45.20 per term. How much does it cost for 4 terms (a full year)?
$45.20 × 4 = ?
$180.80 🚌
$45 × 4 = $180, then $0.20 × 4 = $0.80. Add: $180.80
Problem 4 · Savings Goal 🐖
You want to save $500. You put $15.50 into your account each week. After 10 weeks, how much have you saved?
$15.50 × 10 = ?
$155.00 🐖
× 10 is easy! Move the decimal: $155.00. Still $345 to go!

✅ Repeated purchases: multiply price × quantity (e.g. 25 pizzas × $10.50)

✅ Always write a number sentence first to organise your thinking
⭐ Best value: calculate the total for each option, then compare!
