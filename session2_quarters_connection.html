Introduction: Cents Are Hundredths of a Dollar
Understanding the 100-to-1 relationship between cents and dollars.
The key relationship
💯
$1 = 100 cents
One dollar is exactly one hundred cents. So each cent is one hundredth of a dollar — that's where the second decimal place comes from!
🔗
Cents → Decimals: divide by 100
To convert cents to dollars, divide by 100. To convert dollars to cents, multiply by 100.

⭐ Quarters are special! 25¢ = $0.25 = ¼ of a dollar. Four quarters make exactly one dollar.
Why is $0.25 = ¼?
25 cents out of 100 cents = 25/100
Simplify by dividing top and bottom by 25:  25 ÷ 25 = 1   and   100 ÷ 25 = 4
So   25/100 = 1/4   ✓

Wrap-up: Why Quarters are Special
Let's connect everything we've learned today.
Why do we pay special attention to 25 cents as ¼ of a dollar?
✅ Exactly! The quarter is special for three reasons:

1. It's a common, real coin — we use 25¢ coins every day, so understanding them matters.
2. Fractions connection — $0.25 = 25/100 = 1/4 links decimal money notation directly to fractions.
3. Mental maths — knowing 4 × $0.25 = $1 makes calculating with money much faster!
Why quarters matter — 4 big reasons
🪙
Real-world coin
25¢ is a coin we use every day. Understanding it helps with everyday money situations.
½
Fraction bridge
$0.25 = 1/4 links decimal notation to fractions. Same number, two different ways to write it.
⚡
Mental maths shortcut
4 quarters = $1, 8 quarters = $2, 20 quarters = $5. Quick multiplication becomes easy!
📐
Connects place value
The hundredths column in decimals is the cents column — quarters sit neatly at 25 hundredths.
